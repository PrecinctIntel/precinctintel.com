📍 Local Issue Pulse
Property Taxes and State Spending
🔥 HIGH HEAT — All three candidates have addressed taxes, but with different specificity. Breugelmans has made anti-tax messaging her central theme, pledging she will 'NEVER vote for a tax hike' and attacking the October 2025 legislative session for passing utility rate and tax increases (Facebook, Oct. 31). She specifically targets the 'billion dollar Chicago bailout' and frames suburban taxpayers as victims of Chicago-centric fiscal policy. Penman echoes the tax-reduction message — 'lowering taxes' is listed as her top priority — but has not made specific pledges or identified specific legislation. Clark's website frames her approach as 'responsible leadership and practical solutions' without specific tax pledges. ANALYSIS: In a collar-county district where property tax burden is a perennial top concern, the specificity gap between candidates on this issue is notable. Breugelmans' explicit 'no tax hike' pledge creates a clear accountability marker that neither opponent has matched. (Facebook campaign activity data; jessicaforillinois.com; daniellepenman.com; micheleforilsenate.com)
Education Policy and Parental Rights
🔥 HIGH HEAT — Breugelmans and Penman diverge significantly on education framing. Breugelmans — a former teacher, homeschool parent, and former school board candidate — centers parental rights, education transparency, protection of girls' sports, and school choice. She endorsed Dan Ugaste's position on federal school choice opt-in programs and opposes homeschool regulation legislation. Penman emphasizes career pathways and trades education, citing her visit to the Bricklayers training center and advocating for apprenticeships as alternatives to four-year degrees. Clark's website mentions 'good schools' and her experience running an early childhood learning center, but no specific education policy positions are documented. ANALYSIS: Breugelmans' education platform is the most detailed and ideologically specific in the field, drawing on personal experience as a homeschool parent and former school board candidate. Penman's trades-pathway emphasis appeals to a different constituency and aligns with her labor endorsements. (Facebook campaign activity data; jessicaforillinois.com; daniellepenman.com; micheleforilsenate.com)
