📊 District Profile
Demographics
District 13 Overview — Geneva / St. Charles / Elburn Corridor
Kane County Board District 13 covers portions of Geneva, St. Charles, and Elburn — a predominantly suburban/exurban corridor. Median household income ~$92K (above Kane County average of $83K). Owner-occupied housing rate ~78%. Property tax is structurally the dominant voter issue. Registered voters: approximately 18,000. Comparable primary (2022): 14% turnout — means ~2,500 votes decide this race. (U.S. Census ACS 2020; Kane County Clerk voter registration data)
Property Tax Context
Property Tax — The Defining Issue in D13
All four candidates in District 13 are running explicit anti-tax-increase platforms. The differentiation isn't position — it's credibility. Linder's Finance Committee seat gives him institutional standing. Deniz's business ownership gives him economic credibility. Jimenez and Catella are running on challenger narratives without comparable institutional backing. In a race where everyone agrees on the issue, the candidate who can best credibly claim they'll actually act on it wins the tax-voter.
Turnout Pattern
Low-Turnout Primary — ~2,500 Votes Decides This Race
Based on 2022 comparable primary turnout (~14%), approximately 2,500 total votes will be cast across both primaries in D13. D-side: Linder vs. Jimenez will likely split ~1,400 votes. R-side: Deniz vs. Catella will split ~1,100 votes. Door-knocking and targeted voter contact have outsized impact in races this small. A candidate who moves 200 persuadable voters wins. (Kane County Clerk; 2022 primary turnout analysis)
