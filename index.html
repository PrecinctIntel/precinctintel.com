What Precinct Does

Not a press clipping service.
An intelligence operation.

Every week, Precinct tracks candidates, entities, endorsements, and media coverage across your race. Then it finds the patterns — endorsement anomalies, timing signals, financial infrastructure gaps — that don't show up in any single article.

🔗
Cross-Source Connections

Endorsement gaps, financial anomalies, and messaging patterns that only appear when you read everything at once. Invisible in any single article. Obvious when you read them all.

📊
Week-Over-Week Deltas

Per-candidate deltas on endorsements, media mentions, and finance position. Directional signals every week — so you see the trajectory, not just the snapshot.

⚠️
Anomaly Detection

Campaign infrastructure without financial disclosure. Domain squatting on your name. Institutional endorsements without matching financial infrastructure. Timing patterns that suggest coordinated opposition.

✅
Every Claim Cited

Every insight links to its source. Precision Policy: if we can't verify it, we call it a gap — not a fact.

🏅
Coverage Grade

Every briefing is graded A–F based on source availability for your race. You always know how much we know — and how much we don't.

Sources include: IL SBE Campaign Disclosure, Shaw Local, Patch, Chicago Tribune, The Illinoize, county clerk records, and candidate websites. County board briefings add village board minutes, local Facebook groups, and NextDoor.

The Briefing

Up to eleven sections. Hours of context, compressed into five minutes.

Every briefing follows a consistent structure for its race type. Thin weeks get shorter briefings — we never pad.

Briefing Sections — Weekly Format
  • 👁 What to Watch Cross-source connection insights. The things you didn't know and couldn't have found on your own.
  • 📍 Issue Pulse Which issues are heating up, cooling off, or being owned by which candidate.
  • 🏛 Board Dynamics Who votes with whom. Committee assignments, recent resolutions, and bloc analysis. (county board races)
  • 👤 Candidate Activity This week's moves: endorsements, statements, infrastructure signals, social activity.
  • 📰 Media Mentions Coverage volume, tone signals, and outlet tier. Who's getting covered, where it ran, and whether it's likely to move anything.
  • 💬 Community Pulse Local Facebook groups, NextDoor, and village board minutes — the sources that matter when there's no press coverage. (county board races)
  • 🌐 National Climate How national approval numbers and sentiment connect to your specific race type. (state legislative races)
  • 📊 District Deep Dive Rotating weekly: turnout history, prior margins, demographics, and why it matters this cycle.
  • ⏱ Countdown Days to key milestones. Early voting, filing deadlines, primary and general dates.
  • 📅 Events & Deadlines Candidate forums, filing deadlines, early vote windows — all sourced from official records.
  • 🔍 Intelligence Gaps What we don't know yet. Honest about limits. Gaps that fill in week over week.
Our Policy
You're getting the data, not someone's interpretation of it.

Every source is cited. Every gap is labeled. We don't editorialize, frame, or filter intelligence around a candidate's interests. You get what we found — nothing added, nothing removed.

Every subscriber in the same race receives identical base intelligence. We don't advise. We don't strategize. The moment we start shaping intel around a candidate's needs, we become a campaign consultant. That's a different business with different ethics. We're not that.

Your opponent could subscribe tomorrow and get the same briefing. The value isn't exclusivity. It's accuracy, consistency, and the hours you get back every week.
